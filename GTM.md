## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

We are not targeting generic "startups." We are hunting specific decision-makers who feel the pain of capital burn.

| Attribute         | Detail                                                                                                                                                  |
| :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Job Title**     | CTO, VP of Engineering, or Technical Founder.                                                                                                           |
| **Company Stage** | Series A to Series C (50–300 employees). At this stage, teams are large enough for tool sprawl to occur, but usually lack a dedicated procurement team. |
| **The Trigger**   | They just looked at their corporate Ramp/Brex card statement and saw $15k going to OpenAI/Anthropic without understanding who is using what.            |

## 2. Where They Hang Out (The Watering Holes)

Our users do not search Google for "How to save money on AI tools." They actively discuss engineering scaling and runway preservation in closed communities:

1. **Hacker News (YCombinator):** The epicenter of tech leadership.
2. **Private Slack Communities:** E.g., _CTO Craft_, _Rands Leadership Slack_, _YCombinator Alumni networks_.
3. **Twitter / X:** Niche lists curated around "Engineering Leadership" and "Bootstrapped SaaS."

## 3. The "Zero Budget" 30-Day Playbook (Getting the first 100 users)

We will use a high-value, insight-driven wedge strategy to acquire users without spending a dime on ads.

### Week 1: The "Show me your stack" Wedge

**Action:** We post a highly contrarian, data-backed thread on Twitter/X and IndieHackers.
**The Hook:** _"Stop buying ChatGPT Enterprise. 80% of Series A startups are wasting $2,000/month on ghost seats and the wrong AI tiers. We analyzed 50 stacks. Here is what we found."_
**The CTA:** _"I built a free surgical calculator to see if your engineering team is leaking capital. Drop a '💸' and I'll DM you the link."_ (This bypasses algorithmic link-penalties and drives engagement).

### Week 2: The "Show HN" Launch

**Action:** Launch on Hacker News.
**The Title:** `Show HN: DexAudit - Find out if you are overpaying for your AI dev stack`
**The Strategy:** HN hates marketing speak but loves utility. The post will focus entirely on the mathematical engine: _"We got tired of managing 5 different AI tool subscriptions and realizing half our devs weren't using their seats. I built a Next.js tool that cross-references your team size against official plan minimums to find arbitrage."_

### Week 3: Cold Outbound via "Free Value"

**Action:** Targeted Cold DMs on LinkedIn/X to 200 Engineering Leaders.
**The Template:**

> _"Hey [Name], saw [Company] just hit 50 engineers. Congrats! At that size, most teams accidentally overpay for AI licenses like Cursor and Claude due to overlapping team plans. I built a free, local-first calculator to audit this. It takes 10 seconds and requires no login. Might save you a few grand a month. Check it out: dexaudit.com"_

## 4. The Unfair Distribution Channel

**Credex's Existing Ecosystem.**
While competitors have to buy attention, DexAudit can leverage Credex’s existing secondary market liquidity pool.

**The Strategy:** When Credex partners with VC firms (e.g., Sequoia, a16z) to offer discounted credits to their portfolios, DexAudit becomes the **"Entry Gate."** VCs mandate their portfolio companies to run a DexAudit scan to prove they are capital efficient before unlocking further Credex discounts. This creates an infinite, high-trust, B2B viral loop.
